MVP, Roadmap, Delivery & Commercial Terms

Prepared by Tarun

MVP — what it contains

  • Discovery: study areas → exams → institutes → programs; fees from payment plans where set
  • Apply: authenticated flow — structured application (profile, academics, study mode, etc.)
  • Pay: integrated checkout (e.g. Razorpay) with a clear production policy for demo/staging modes
  • Post-payment: confirmation with enrollment reference; partner institutes → enrollment path; non-partner → qualified lead handoff (same student UX)
  • Account: “My applications” with status and payment context
  • Deferred for later phase: document uploads, full admin/CMS self-service (unless scoped in)

Phased feature plan

  • Phase 1 — MVP launch: catalog, apply, pay, confirmations, partner vs non-partner logic, student account
  • Phase 2 — Operations: content tools or CMS, institute onboarding workflows, notifications, richer reporting
  • Phase 3 — Scale: document verification, CRM/deep integrations, analytics dashboards, automation

Phases are sequenced to control cost, risk, and time-to-value — depth agreed per phase.

How we create the application

  • Delivery model: iterative builds on a shared staging environment; sign-off gates before production
  • Architecture: modern web app + API + database; secure sessions; validated forms end-to-end
  • Quality: structured schemas for data, test-friendly flows, documented configuration (e.g. payments, origins)
  • Handover: repository access, deployment notes, and operational runbook for your team or hosting partner

Detailed technical appendix available separately for engineering stakeholders.

Timeline & maintenance

  • Timeline: fixed after questionnaire + design/content readiness; typical MVP ranges from several weeks to a few months depending on integrations and legal/payment readiness
  • Maintenance (proposed): defined term post go-live (e.g. 3–6 months) covering agreed bug fixes, security patches, and environment health
  • Exclusions (unless added): new features, major redesigns, third-party outages, day-to-day content entry
  • Optional: extended support, SLAs, monitoring, and on-call — as add-ons

Requirement questionnaire (what we need)

  • Catalog & commercial: programs, fees, payment plans, partner vs non-partner rules per institute
  • Brand & content: copy, imagery, legal pages, refund/cancellation policy ownership
  • Payments: Razorpay (or other) account, KYC, who receives settlements, test vs live timelines
  • Operations: who handles student queries, institute coordination, lead routing for non-partners
  • Compliance: data retention, consent, minors/parent data — legal counsel engagement as needed
  • Infrastructure: domain, hosting preferences, admin users, launch date constraints

Cost to company

  • MVP build: fixed fee or phased quotes based on signed scope (this deck + questionnaire)
  • Recurring: hosting, domains, SSL, email/SMS if used, payment gateway fees — typically your accounts
  • Change requests: estimated additively when scope moves beyond agreed MVP/phase

Specific numbers provided in a formal proposal after requirements workshop.

Payment plan

  • Milestone 1 — Kickoff: discovery signed; access to stakeholders & materials
  • Milestone 2 — Design / scope lock: UX & technical scope approved
  • Milestone 3 — MVP on staging: end-to-end demo for UAT
  • Milestone 4 — Go-live: production cutover after acceptance
  • Maintenance: monthly or bundled per agreed term

Percent splits and invoicing dates aligned to your procurement process.

Success metrics & ownership

  • Metrics: applications started/completed, payment conversion, partner enrollments vs non-partner leads, time-to-confirm
  • RACI (summary): you own catalog accuracy, institute relationships, student support policy; we own build, deployment mechanics, and agreed defect fixes
  • Joint: UAT sign-off, launch communications, payment go-live checklist

Go-live readiness

  • Production hosting, backups, domain & SSL
  • Live payment gateway certification; demo checkout disabled in production per policy
  • Environment separation (staging vs production)
  • Trust & compliance: student/parent data handling, consent, and published policies — aligned with your legal counsel
  • Monitoring and incident basics — scoped under maintenance or infra add-on

Risks & dependencies

  • Late institute agreements or incomplete program/fee data
  • Payment provider approval timelines
  • Legal/policy delays affecting copy and data practices
  • Scope creep without change control — mitigated by phased roadmap
