Proposal for Cosmetic Brand Website

End-to-end website design and development — brand presence, six-product showcase, and e-commerce capability — delivered by Maple AI Technologies.

1. Project overview

Mr. Jatinder Singh is launching a new cosmetic business with an initial catalogue of six products. The objective of this engagement is to deliver a professional, conversion-focused website that establishes the brand's digital presence, showcases all six products with compelling detail, and enables direct sales through a seamless checkout experience.

The site will be mobile-first and fully responsive, reflecting the reality that the majority of cosmetic buyers discover and purchase via smartphones. The design language will align with the brand's identity — clean, modern, and trustworthy — communicating quality and authenticity from the first visit.

Maple AI Technologies will own the full delivery lifecycle: UI/UX design, development, content integration, payment setup, and a go-live hand-off with a structured support window. Post-launch, an optional annual maintenance contract keeps the site secure, fast, and up to date.

2. Platforms covered

Customers will interact through a fully responsive website optimised for mobile, tablet, and desktop browsers. The brand team will manage products, orders, and content through a browser-based admin dashboard — no technical knowledge required.

3. Website scope — Phase 1

Each page has a clear role from discovery to purchase or enquiry.

Home page

Hero, primary CTA, product grid for all six items, short brand story, and trust signals.

Product pages — six products

Dedicated detail per product: imagery, copy, ingredients, usage, variants, add to cart, related items — editable from admin.

Shop / product listing page

Grid or list of all six products with filter/sort and fast mobile performance.

About / brand story page

Founders, values, sourcing, and differentiation for first-time buyers.

Cart and checkout

Cart, address, payment (UPI / card / COD via agreed gateway), confirmation, and order email.

Contact page

Contact form, social links, optional WhatsApp; wholesale tagged via subject if needed.

Legal and policy pages

Privacy, shipping, returns, and terms — template draft from Maple AI; client signs off before go-live.

4. Admin panel — scope

Browser-based console for operations without ongoing developer dependency.

Product management

Add, edit, publish/unpublish — images, copy, prices, stock, and variants.

Order management

Status queue, internal notes, and customer notifications at agreed milestones.

Customer and enquiry records

Customers and form submissions; CSV export for fulfilment or finance.

Basic discount and coupon management

Percentage or fixed codes with optional expiry and usage caps.

5. Technology stack (indicative)

Frontend

React / Next.js for performance, SEO-oriented rendering, and modern UI.

E-commerce layer

Custom cart/orders or lightweight headless commerce — confirmed at kick-off.

Admin console

Browser SPA with role-based access for non-technical users.

Database

PostgreSQL with ORM (e.g. Prisma) for catalogue, orders, and customers.

Payment

Razorpay or equivalent — UPI, cards, net banking, plus COD flag.

Email

Transactional email (e.g. Resend or SendGrid) for orders and status updates.

Hosting

Cloud deployment with separate staging and production.

Media

Image optimisation and CDN for fast mobile product loads.

6. Delivery plan

  • Week 1 — Discovery & Design: brand onboarding, wireframes, UI mockups for key pages (Home, Product, Checkout), client review and sign-off.
  • Week 2–3 — Development — Core: home page, shop, all six product pages, about, contact, legal pages; admin product and order management.
  • Week 4 — Development — Checkout & Payments: cart, checkout flow, payment gateway integration, order confirmation emails, discount codes.
  • Week 5 — QA & Content Integration: full QA across devices and browsers; content population (product copy, images, policies); performance and SEO checks.
  • Week 6 — Go-Live & Hand-Off: staging sign-off, DNS / domain setup, production deployment, admin training, and hand-off documentation.

Total duration: 6 weeks from receipt of brand assets and signed statement of work. Timeline assumes timely client feedback (within 2 business days at each review stage).

7. Key assumptions

  • Client supplies photography and logo.
  • Product copy (written content for products — names, descriptions, ingredients, claims on the site) is client-provided and approved before product-page build.
  • Client owns the domain; Maple AI assists with DNS.
  • Client completes payment-gateway KYC/merchant setup; Maple AI assists in merchant setup; Maple AI handles technical integration.
  • Policy text is client- or counsel-approved before go-live.
  • Post–design-sign-off changes go through change control.

8. Out of scope (unless added by change request)

  • Custom mobile application (iOS / Android) — can be scoped as Phase 2.
  • Subscription or membership models beyond standard one-time purchase.
  • Multi-vendor marketplace functionality.
  • Loyalty points or rewards programme.
  • Advanced analytics dashboards or BI reporting beyond basic order data export.
  • Social media content creation, ad campaigns, or SEO copywriting (brand content stays client-owned).
  • Third-party logistics (3PL) system integration — can be scoped as Phase 2.
